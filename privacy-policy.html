01 - Overview

CyberBlock - Ad Eliminator is built on a single principle: zero data collection. We do not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal information about you or your browsing activity.

This Privacy Policy explains how CyberBlock - Ad Eliminator ("the extension", "we") handles your data when you install and use it in Google Chrome.

02 - Data We Do NOT Collect

CyberBlock never collects or transmits any of the following:

  • Browsing history or visited URLs
  • Personally identifiable information (name, email, IP address)
  • Login credentials or session tokens
  • Financial or payment information
  • Device identifiers or fingerprint data
  • Cookies or local storage from websites you visit
  • Search queries or form inputs
  • Analytics, telemetry, or crash reports

03 - How the Extension Works

CyberBlock operates entirely on your local device. Here is what it does:

  • Intercepts and blocks network requests matching known ad, tracker, malware, and popup domains using Chrome's built-in declarativeNetRequest API
  • Injects content scripts on web pages solely to remove ad elements from the DOM
  • Periodically fetches updated blocking rule lists from GitHub (raw JSON files) to keep protection current - only the rule files are downloaded, no user data is sent
  • Stores your on/off toggle preferences locally using chrome.storage.local - this data never leaves your device
  • Counts blocked ads locally for display in the popup - this counter is stored only on your device

04 - Permissions Explained

  • declarativeNetRequest - blocks ads and trackers at the network level without reading page content
  • storage - saves your settings and blocked-ad counters locally on your device only
  • alarms - schedules periodic rule update checks (every 6 hours)
  • tabs - reads the current tab's URL to show per-site blocking status in the popup
  • host permissions (*://*/*) - required to apply blocking rules across all websites

No permission is used to read, record, or transmit your browsing data.

05 - Rule Updates (GitHub)

CyberBlock fetches updated ad-blocking rule lists from:

https://github.com/mgupta646/cyberblock-rules

These requests download plain JSON rule files only. No user data, identifiers, or browsing information is included in these requests. GitHub may log standard server-side access logs (IP address, timestamp) as part of normal CDN operation - this is outside our control and subject to GitHub's Privacy Policy.

06 - Third-Party Services

CyberBlock does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. The extension has no backend server, no user accounts, and no sign-in requirement.

07 - Children's Privacy

CyberBlock does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13. Since no data is collected at all, the extension is safe for users of all ages.
